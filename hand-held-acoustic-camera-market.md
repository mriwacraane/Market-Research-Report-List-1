<p><p>The hand-held acoustic camera market features two primary types of microphones: MEMS microphones and traditional array microphones. MEMS microphones are compact, offering high sensitivity and directivity, making them ideal for capturing sound in tight spaces. In contrast, traditional array microphones consist of multiple microphones arranged in a specific pattern, providing enhanced spatial resolution and directionality. Both types cater to various applications, such as industrial diagnostics, environmental monitoring, and sound source localization, enhancing the overall capability of acoustic imaging.</p></p>
