<p><ul><li>Anionic Surfactant</li><li>Cationic Surfactant</li><li>Amphoteric Surfactant</li><li>Nonionic Surfactant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The surfactant market is categorized into four main types: Anionic surfactants, which carry a negative charge and are commonly used in detergents and soaps for their cleaning efficiency; Cationic surfactants, with a positive charge, often found in fabric softeners and disinfectants for their antimicrobial properties; Amphoteric surfactants, which can act as either anionic or cationic, offering mildness and versatility in personal care products; and Nonionic surfactants, which contain no charge and are valued for their stability and low toxicity in various applications.</p></p>
