<p><p>Rotary Friction Welding (RFW) is a solid-state welding process that joins materials by generating heat through the friction between a rotating workpiece and a stationary component. <p><strong>The Rotary Friction Welding Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Inertia Rotary Friction Welding</li><li>Continuous Drive Rotary Friction Welding</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Rotary Friction Welding market is primarily categorized into two types: Inertia Rotary Friction Welding and Continuous Drive Rotary Friction Welding. Inertia Rotary Friction Welding utilizes a rotating component that builds up kinetic energy before being brought into contact with the workpiece, resulting in welding through friction heat. In contrast, Continuous Drive Rotary Friction Welding maintains a constant power source, allowing for continuous rotation and weld formation. Both methods are effective for joining various materials, particularly in industries like automotive and aerospace.</p></p>
