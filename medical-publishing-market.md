<p><p>The medical publishing market consists of two primary models: Open Access and Subscription-based. The Open Access model allows unrestricted online access to research articles, promoting widespread dissemination and collaboration without paywalls. In contrast, the Subscription-based model requires users or institutions to pay fees to access journal content, often providing in-depth research articles but limiting availability. Each model has implications for researchers, institutions, and funding, impacting how medical knowledge is shared and utilized in the healthcare community.</p></p>
