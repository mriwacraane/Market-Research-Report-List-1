<p><p>The bone screws market includes three primary types: titanium, stainless steel, and bioabsorbable screws. Titanium screws are favored for their strength and biocompatibility, making them ideal for long-term fixation. Stainless steel screws offer durability and cost-effectiveness, commonly used in various orthopedic applications. Bioabsorbable screws are designed to dissolve over time, eliminating the need for surgical removal and promoting healing, making them suitable for temporary fixation in pediatric and less invasive surgeries. Each type caters to specific medical needs.</p></p>
